Here With Oca (2026)

Here With Oca is a short film about an angel, Oca, who has fallen from heaven and is being interviewed by Briar, a blog writer and aspiring journalist. The first section of this film would unfold like a highly produced and edited youtube video interview until Briar’s camera loses power at a crucial moment. Then the camera moves “behind the scenes” of Briar’s studio setup as the two characters engage in a more personal conversation.

One major improvement from our initial proof of concept was the lighting we used for this living room scene. We were able to make use of multiple windows and the overall openness of the room to allow the scene to be lit primarily with natural lighting. Then we used some extra lights, including the prop panel lights themselves, to fill some shadows. The bedroom we used for this film was quite large and an unrealistic fit for Briar’s character, so we deliberately kept the camera looking towards only one half of the room to make it seem smaller than it actually was. We also made the lighting slightly warmer here to create a more intimate atmosphere as Oca and Briar’s friendship progressed.

Again, our proximity to a main road made sound recording for outside scenes a pain. To work around this, we made sure to call action after any vehicles had passed and kept the boom angled away from the roads. I also made sure to keep a manual list of each sound clip, making notes on any noises which might have interrupted the take.

I was also able to develop a full original score for the film, replacing the free music we had in our test version. I assigned both characters a specific leitmotif, with the interview theme featuring Briar’s motif and Oca’s theme playing while he was wandering the house. Then as the narrative progressed I combined the leitmotifs of both characters into a single track as they slowly grow closer and become friends. For Oca’s monologue explaining why he left heaven, I used an alternate key and some new instruments such as a droning synth and harp to signify an alien environment. This sound then modulates into a more familiar key and reprises Oca’s theme as his monologue switches from talking about heaven to earth.
