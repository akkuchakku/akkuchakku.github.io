Tracks (In Order)

  1. Briar's Theme

    2. The character, Briar's leitmotif, first introduced in the form of a higher produced soft EDM tune, since this is intended to be the backing track of a youtube video that the character is producing.

  2. Clicks

    3. Syncopated staccato notes in to fill the conversation between the two characters. This supports the uneasy, awkward atmosphere between the two characters who aren't yet familiar with each other.

  3. Oca's Theme

    4. Oca's first moment to himself. We hear his leitmotif in a moderate waltz which creates a sense of movement as a montage plays of Oca wandering the house on his own.

  4. Oca Remembers

    5. A short follow up to Oca's Theme, where the music moves to a slow 4/4 time and sets the tone for the more intimate bedroom setting.

  5. Eternity

    6. This plays over an animated segment narrated by Oca. The track is in 5/4 using two completely new instruments, having modulated into a new dorian key which creates a feeling of unfamiliarity as Oca talks about his time in heaven. Then the music returns to the main key of the soundtrack and reprises the familiar Oca's Theme as his narration shifts to the subject of humanity.

  6. Malleable

    7. Some simple notes over a meaningful conversation, which then ends in a call and response between the two character's motifs as the two characters begin to connect with each other.

  7. B-Roll

    8. This track slowly builds in musical layers and intensity, playing both characters themes simultaneously as they grow closer over the final montage of the film.

  8. Credits

    9. A simplified reprise of the B-Roll track.
