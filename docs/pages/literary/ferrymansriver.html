Ferryman's River

As the first short story I ever wrote for a uni class, this piece was inspired by a random mental image of a woman stumbling through a battlefield and a 200 word scene description that I had written about it years ago. When preparing drafts for this story, I looked back over some of my older scraps of writing and decided to experiment with different ways that I could progress this narrative, such as introducing an afterlife. This text was also partially written in response to some reemerging social tendencies for individuals who held a dogmatic hatred for demonised groups of people. However I did recently change the original ending, as I had come to realise that the conclusion made an uncessecary compromise which undercut the core of its narrative.
