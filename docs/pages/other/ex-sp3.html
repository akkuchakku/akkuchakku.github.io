Class Workshop Exercises

Screen Production III (ARTS3061)

Week 2 - Projects & Teams

This week we had to create a chase sequence inspired by animated comic cartoons. One character was trying to return a forgotten phone, though the other character, wearing headphones, misunderstood the situation and assumed she was being chased. To suggest this, I removed sound for scenes centred around the chased character, playing music instead, until the final scene where their headphones are knocked off and they are able to hear again. We incorporated some cartoon inspired gags in the form of the chaser taking the wrong stairs, both characters somehow reversing roles then realising what had happened, and them fighting with a giant teddy bear between them.

Week 3 - Lighting

For this task we were instructed to recreate scenes from our final project using three different lighting setups. The first scene, we used a single light source, lighting the set with a noir aesthetic which created harsh shadows around the edges of the frame. Following our script, the lighting stand is visible in this frame and the actor playing “Briar” is shadowed as they have left the ‘set’ within the story. The single point of lighting makes the shadows here more apparent, and means that the actor does not have to move far to create the intended contrast. For the second scene we used a three point lighting setup, using the same script so again some of the stands are visible. This scene is lit more evenly and Briar’s actor has to move further away to create some shadows. The final scene was lit under natural lighting, and we used an outdoor section where the intended lighting setup was natural and even.

Week 5 - Blocking & Coverage

In this task we were to film two people playing a simple game with no dialogue, instead using physical and environmental cues to tell the story. For context, in this game both players take turns to swap cards from their hand until someone ends up with a run of four cards, and three cards of the same number. The sequence begins with a simple wide shot of the entire table, and some pov shots of both players. We added some extreme close ups of both characters glancing at each other to signal some rivalry. As the game progresses, the audience can see that both players are one card away from winning. Both are holding two jacks and waiting on a third, which, unknown to them must come from the other player. We then have a short clip of the two players rapidly picking up and placing down cards, unable to find jacks to build tension. Then the game reaches the last card in the draw pile and the pace slows down, as one of the characters considers switching strategy. Then as he swaps the last card for a jack, his opponent realises and takes advantage of his mistake, declaring victory. We also decided to integrate the cup as a physical and not just decorative prop, just to slow down the final shots and create more suspense.

Week 7 - Directing Actors

For this task we were to recreate a dialogue heavy section of our final project but without any sound, attempting to communicate the same meaning as the original through signs and exaggerated acting. The actor for Briar begins scrolling on their phone through a Wikepedia page on angels as in the original, then holds up a picture of some stereotypical pearly gates to Oca in place of asking verbally. Oca shugs replacing his verbal response. He is continuously drawing in this scene to indicate his connection to drawing. Then Briar points to his face, indicating towards the censor covering it. (The censor was not added here since this was not edited by our group’s editor, for the purposes of the exercise we can imagine there is a white circle over his face). Oca draws circles around his face explaining the censor. The Briar laughs and signals to him that they want to see Oca’s face anyway, and Oca responds with gestures that Briar would go insane. Some parts of the dialogue were still lost due to the circumstances of the shot, as this sequence doesn’t show the audience what Oca is drawing, we cant visually see what the purpose of the drawings are. Additionally the fact that Briar wants Oca’s face for a YouTube thumbnail is simplified, and instead we indicated that Briar was just curious to see his face.


Week 8 - Producing

This was a larger production project where we aimed to take a prewritten script for a short film and produce it in one lesson. Since may of the sequences on the script were very simple and made use of basic office settings, we decided to change things up for the receptionist’s office and the rooms of each office worker, such as having the receptionist use absurdly outdated technology, an office worker in a music room or one in a lounge room with multiple books open on a table. Each dialogue section was broken up by the protagonist running through long hallways or up and down stairs, and these running sequences gradually increased in speed and intensity to indicate a growing desperation for the protagonist, and to heighten audience tension. To increase comedic effect, we added the ‘denied access’ sound from the uni key card readers, at the climax of the rising tension as the protagonist finds the out of order sign on the bathroom. We recorded the sound of a tap for the final scene and after class feedback, adjusted the disgusted reaction shot from the receptionist to be quicker and more connected to the previous shot.
