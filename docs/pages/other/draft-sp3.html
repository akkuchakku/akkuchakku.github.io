Here With Oca (Single Location Proof of Concept)

Our scriptwriter originally intended to create this as a personal project, so from the beginning he had a strong vision for what the final short film should look like. This substantially helped us with the development of creative production documents, as we were able to repurpose the frames of a sketch animation that the scriptwriter had made as reference material to create our storyboard. Each group member additionally reached out to friends and family members to secure actors, a location and backups for both as soon as possible.

The most prominent creative feature of this proof of concept was the transition between diegetic interview footage and a more realistic visual after the main character, Briar’s “in universe” camera looses power. To make this shift more visually apparent, we used a tracking shot showing Briar walk past the studio lights which lit the previous “interview” shot to a darker section of the room with more natural lighting, creating a dramatic contrast.
To make the process of filming this shot easier, our group decided to record this earlier in the day, to leave time for revisions and retakes, as well as practising camera and actor movement beforehand.

A significant challenge regarding sound recording were these construction vehicles just outside the main window to our filming location. While closing the window helped dampen the sound, I made sure to keep the mic angled away from the window and focused on the actors. We also used lav mics as extra backup sound, since both characters already had visible mics as props.
